I am a fourth-year Computer Science PhD candidate at the [University of Maryland, College Park](https://www.cs.umd.edu/), advised by [Prof. Nirupam Roy](https://www.cs.umd.edu/~nirupam/) in the [iCoSMoS Lab](http://icosmos.cs.umd.edu/).

My research builds **physics-informed machine learning** systems for **spatial acoustics, RF sensing, and ambient computing** — enabling rich perception and inference on **wearable and low-power platforms** embedded in everyday objects and environments. I am interested in how resource-constrained, intermittently-powered systems can perceive gestures, micro-motions, directions, and physical context for environment-sensing and ubiquitous-healthcare applications.

I have been fortunate to intern at [Dolby Laboratories](https://www.dolby.com/) (San Francisco), where I worked on modelling car-cabin acoustic frequency-response fields with implicit neural representations, and at [Nokia Bell Labs](https://www.bell-labs.com/) (Cambridge, UK) in the Pervasive Systems group on detecting auditory attention from in-ear muscle contractions on earables. Earlier, I was a Lead Researcher at [Silence Laboratories](https://silencelaboratories.com/) (Singapore). I received my B.E. from [BITS Pilani](https://www.bits-pilani.ac.in/) in 2020.

**Research interests:** Spatial Acoustics · Physics-informed Machine Learning · Multimodal Imaging · RF Sensing · Ambient Computing · Ubiquitous Healthcare · Low-power Learning Systems

Feel free to reach out by [email](mailto:htakawal@umd.edu) — I'm always happy to chat about research, collaborations, or internships.
